Travel

Portage Lake - Waterloo Recreation Area is a 15 minute drive from Jackson, Michigan going East on I-94, not far from exit 147 (Race Rd) on Seymour Dr.

From Saginaw - Portage Lake is about 2 hours south via M-52 or via I-75 and US-23 to I-94W.

Directions from Jackson and West:
Take I‐94 to exit 153 (Race Road). Go North on Race Road 3 miles to Seymour Rd. Turn right and travel 1 mile on Seymour Rd. Park entrance on left side of road.

Park Entrance to Portage Lake Area

** IMPORTANT** For entrance into the Park
All vehicles entering the Recreation Area need a “Recreation Passport” . PLEASE BRING THE COUPON ENCLOSED WITH YOUR INVITATION. When you present the coupon at the entrance booth you will receive a complimentary Recreation Passport.

If you have forgotten your coupon when you arrive please let them know you are there for the Hay/Konkle wedding reception to receive your complimentary Recreation Passport. But to ensure an easy entry into the park, please have your coupon available. Only one coupon per vehicle is needed. Photocopies of the coupon are fine.

(If you are a Michigan resident you may have purchased a Recreation Passport when you renewed your license plate and you will have an indicative sticker on your plate. If you do not already have one, your complimentary Recreation Passport will be valid for any Michigan State Park or Recreation Area until your next plate renewal.)

Attire

Wear casual, fun, summer attire geared toward your comfort and love of good celebration. You may also want to bring swimwear and towels for use at the beach.

Kids

We are hoping that kids of all ages will be joining us and celebrating with us. There are two very nice playgrounds about 450 feet from our pavilion and a sandy beach about 700 feet away. Grassy areas surround the pavilion for running and romping and lawn games. An 18 hole frisbee golf course and a volleyball court are also near the pavilion. There is a fishing pier and rental row boats nearby on the lake as well as wooded natural areas and a one mile loop nature trail. The pavilion has attached bathrooms.
